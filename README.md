World Happiness Analysis 2019
This project analyses happiness data from 156 countries to identify the factors most strongly associated with a country's happiness score. Using SQL for data querying and ranking logic, Excel for visualisation and reporting, and PowerPoint to present the outcome. The analysis explores the relationship between happiness and variables such as GDP per capita, social support, healthy life expectancy, freedom to make life choices, generosity, and perceptions of corruption.

Key questions explored:
  Are the happiest countries also the wealthiest by GDP per capita?
  Does perception of corruption have a measurable relationship with happiness?
  Which countries have the largest gap between their GDP ranking and happiness ranking?
  How much do social support and freedom to make life choices contribute to happiness?

Tools used: 
  SQL (querying, ranking, aggregation) 
  Excel (data visualisation, dashboard reporting)
  PowerPoint (presenting the results)
